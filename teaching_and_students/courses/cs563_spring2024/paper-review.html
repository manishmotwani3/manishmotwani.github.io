This is an individual assignment. Each student is to submit one review for every paper we discuss in class. The write-up should consider the discussions about the paper presented on Canvas and Q/A after the paper presentation during class time.

Late assignments will not be accepted without prior permission.

Overview

The goal of this assignment is to write a review of the papers presented during the class.

See these notes by John Ousterhout on writing reviews. Focus on the positive: these are classics for a reason!

Here are two example-reviews-1.pdf for the paper coz.pdf: 

  1. The first is a real conference review.
  2. The second is a real student review for this class that you should use a model.

Your reviews must address the following points:

  • Summary: (at least two paragraphs)
    What is the problem that this work addressed?
    What are the big ideas / key insights / technical contributions?
    How is the solution evaluated?
  • Significance/Contributions:  
    What is the practical significance of these results today?
    How likely are the assumptions / context this work was based on going to change in the future?
  • Discussion Points: 
    Include at least three discussion points that you found interesting that could describe strengths or weaknesses of the paper.

 

Deliverables

Each student should upload 1 pdf file (<paper-id>_review_lastname.pdf) via Canvas. There is no right length minimum or maximum:  you should write enough to describe the Summary, Significance/Contributions, and Discussion Points.
